Overview

My journey (idk sounds cheesy)

My journey with rubik's cubes started when I got my first rubik's cube for my birthday.
I was still in middle school at the time and I messed around with it pretending to scramble it and undoing the moves pretending to solve it.
Then it sat on a shelf collecting dust for a while.
Then one day I picked it up again and pretended to solve it, but I messed up.
Now I had a scrambled cube and was panicing.
I thought about peeling the stickers off like I'd heard other people do but I decided that was too much of a copout.
So I went on youtube and searched up a way to solve it.
The tutorials I found went way over my head, it had notation I didn't understand and long algorithms that looked like gibberish.
But I also discoverd you could just disassemble the cube and reassemble it in a solved state.
For a while this was the only way I knew how to get my cube back into a solved state.
I eventually got tired of doing this and got curious on how do people acutally solve this thing.
I went back on the internet again and I found that the official Rubik's brand website had a tutorial and I decieded to give it a go again.
It had helpful pictures and diagrams to help me learn the cube notation and had step by step pictures for each move of the algorithms.
It took a couple days and plenty of frustration but I eventually, finally, solved the cube.
Fast forward a few years after learning improved solving methods and lots of new algorithms, I'd gotten my solve time down to below a minute.
At present if I really focus up after some warming up I can pretty easily get below 45-50 seconds.
