Cube anatomy

First we're going to need to go over some cube anatomy. A 3x3 cube is made up of three types of pieces: corners, edges, and centers. Centers have one face, edges have two, and corners have 3 faces and the centers are physically attached together by the core.

This means that the orientation of the faces is always static. That's to say the white is always opposite yellow, the red is always opposite orange, the blue is always opposite green. Some models might look slightly different but they're all the same in concept

Cube Notation

In order to learn algorithms (sequences of moves) to do specific actions on specific pieces we first need to go over how they're written. We use cube notation to denote which face needs to be moved and in which direction.

For example an F would mean that we should turn the front face clockwise, while an F' (said as: F prime) means we should turn the front face counter clockwise.

By using this notation we can all be speaking the same language to describe the algorithms that we come up with.

CFOP

CFOP is the most widely used method for solving the 3x3 rubik's cube
it's an acronym for: Cross (completing the white cross) F2L (completing the first two layers) OLL (orienting last layer) PLL (permuting last layer)
The beginner's (layer by layer) method that the Rubik's Brand website uses for their "You can solve the cube" instructions, it's basically CFOP on training wheels.

Provided is a playlist made by J Perm, a popular youtuber in the cubing space, that goes over a beginner's method in video form.
