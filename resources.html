Cube anatomy

first we're going to need to go over some cube anatomy
a 3x3 cube is made up of three types of pieces: corners, edges, and centers.
the centers are physically attached together by the core.
this means that the orientation of the faces is always static.
that's to say the white is always opposite yellow, the red is always opposite orange, the blue is always opposite green.
some models might looks slightly different but they're all the same in concept
centers have one face, edges have two, and corners have 3 faces.

cfop

CFOP is the most widely used method for solving the 3x3 rubik's cube
it's an acronym for Cross (completing the white cross) F2L (completing the first two layers) OLL (orienting last layer) PLL (permuting last layer)
it's very similar in structure to the beginner's method that the Rubik's Brand website uses for their "You can solve the cube" instructions.
